Why I quit Facebook

The other day, I authored a blog post entitled Stop Driving Now, which was effectively an angry rant fueled by the actions of aggressive motorists on my ride home that afternoon. Smugly, I posted it on Facebook, hoping it would attract attention. It did. I was ostracized (rightly) as an angry asshole. Comment after comment filled the link post until I finally decided to delete it. But another thing happened: I became consumed with the opinions of my peers. I had a hard time sleeping that night because I knew that my post had offended someone. And it was far from the first time this had happened.

A few weeks earlier, I encountered a post by an acquaintance thinking of joining the Marines. Being in staunch opposition to joining the military as a career choice, I voiced my concern with hope that I might change her mind. After all, the opinion of a relative stranger couldn't hurt, could it?

It could. An hour and 30 comments later, I had alienated myself from people I hadn't even met. Despite never having shared an experience to substantiate resistance, I created a situation which left everyone feeling exhausted and vulnerable.

Social media is my generation's sitcoms. Replace Jerry, Elaine, George and Kramer with four people you met at a bar a year ago, and you've effectively captured the level of triviality our Facebook feeds provide us. Even seemingly noble pursuits, whether the JCPenney fathers day ad or the Oatmeal fundraiser, have no bearing on the life we lead outside of Facebook and are better digested outside a public forum.

I've chosen to take the red pill and see how deep the rabbit hole of real life goes. See you on the other side.

If you're ready to take the plunge and delete your Facebook account permanently, visit this page in the Facebook Help Center: How do I permanently delete my account?
